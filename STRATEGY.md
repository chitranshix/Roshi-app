# Roshi — Strategy

---

## Problem

Most people want a better vocabulary but don't know how to build one. The tools that exist — dictionaries, flashcards, word-of-the-day apps — are passive. You look up a word, feel smart for a moment, and forget it within days. Passive exposure doesn't create retention. Using a word does.

---

## Mission

Make vocabulary something you build actively — through use, through people, every day.

---

## Vision

A world where growing your vocabulary feels as natural as checking your phone.

---

## What Roshi is

A daily vocabulary game with a social layer. You learn words by playing with them — in sentences, in definitions, under pressure — alone or with friends. The social mechanic creates stakes. Stakes create memory.

---

## Who it's for

18–35 year olds who are ambitious about how they communicate — students, early-career professionals, writers, readers. People who already care about words but don't have a good way to build on that. The only requirement is curiosity.

---

## Positioning

**What we are:** The most engaging way to build vocabulary — social by design, effective alone.

**What we are not:** A dictionary. A study tool. A flashcard app. An education product.

**The wedge:** Most vocabulary tools optimise for coverage (how many words can we show you). Roshi optimises for retention (how many words will you actually remember).

---

## Core beliefs

1. **You only own a word when you can use it.** Recognition is not retention. Production is.
2. **Social pressure is a feature.** Being called out by a friend for getting a word wrong is more memorable than any flashcard.
3. **Daily is the only habit that compounds.** A little every day beats a lot occasionally.
4. **Fun and effective are not in tension.** The best learning products don't feel like learning.

---

## Goals (near-term)

- Build a daily habit for users — make the daily word the first thing they open in the morning
- Grow word-of-mouth through the dare/challenge loop — every dare is an invite
- Reach 1,000 DAU before expanding the word library or adding new mechanics

---

## What success looks like

| Signal | Target |
|--------|--------|
| D7 retention | > 40% |
| Avg. session length | > 3 min |
| Dares sent per DAU | > 0.5 |
| Streak (7+ days) | > 20% of active users |

---

## What we're not solving (yet)

- Formal vocabulary testing or certification
- Language learning (non-English)
- Classroom / institutional use
